About Us

The Institution Behind the Awards

The Capital Health Awards Academy is Nigeria's foremost platform for recognising excellence, integrity, and innovation in healthcare — born from over 70 episodes of public health advocacy.

Our Mission

To institutionalise the formal recognition of healthcare excellence in Nigeria — ensuring that outstanding service, innovation, and integrity within the healthcare ecosystem are acknowledged through a credible, transparent, and nationally respected platform.

Our Vision

To become the foremost platform for healthcare recognition and advocacy across Africa — a continental institution that elevates the standard of healthcare practice through sustained recognition, professional dialogue, and public engagement.

Why This Awards Exists

Through over 70 episodes of The Health Show — broadcast weekly on NTA News 24 since September 2024 — our founding team engaged hundreds of healthcare professionals across Nigeria. What emerged consistently was a clear institutional gap: exceptional healthcare service was rarely formally acknowledged.

Healthcare workers operate at the intersection of national productivity, social stability, and human dignity. Recognition serves as both affirmation and incentive — reinforcing standards while inspiring future generations. The Capital Health Awards exists to fill that gap permanently.

Award Categories

Seven Awards. One Standard. Nigeria's Best.

🩺

Excellence in Clinical Practice

For healthcare professionals who have demonstrated outstanding clinical skill, measurable patient outcomes, and unwavering ethical practice.

Eligibility

Licensed medical practitioners with minimum 5 years clinical experience. Open to physicians, nurses, pharmacists, and allied health professionals.

Evaluation Criteria

Patient outcomes, peer recognition, ethical conduct, professional development, and community impact.

💡

Healthcare Innovation Award

Recognising individuals or institutions who have introduced transformative, evidence-based solutions to Nigeria's healthcare delivery challenges.

Eligibility

Healthcare professionals, researchers, institutions, and health-tech innovators. Both individuals and organisations may apply.

Evaluation Criteria

Innovation scope, demonstrated impact, scalability, sustainability, and measurable improvement in healthcare access or outcomes.

🌍

Public Health Leadership Award

Honouring leaders whose policy work, community engagement, and public health advocacy has measurably improved population health outcomes in Nigeria.

Eligibility

Public health officers, health policy advocates, community health leaders, and NGO health directors.

Evaluation Criteria

Population health impact, policy influence, leadership quality, community reach, and evidence of sustained outcomes.

⭐

Young Healthcare Professional Award

Celebrating early-career professionals (under 35) who have demonstrated exceptional impact, professional integrity, and transformative promise within their discipline.

Eligibility

Licensed healthcare professionals under 35 years of age with a minimum of 2 years professional practice.

Evaluation Criteria

Early-career achievements, leadership potential, innovation, peer recognition, and commitment to professional development.

🏛

Institutional Excellence Award

For healthcare organisations and institutions demonstrating outstanding governance, measurable health outcomes, and sustained contribution to Nigeria's health system strengthening.

Eligibility

Registered hospitals, clinics, NGOs, and healthcare organisations operating in Nigeria for a minimum of 3 years.

Evaluation Criteria

Quality of care, health outcomes, governance standards, community impact, and staff development record.

👑

Lifetime Achievement Award

The most prestigious recognition the Capital Health Awards confers — honouring a career of enduring, transformative contribution to Nigeria's healthcare sector and national wellbeing.

This award is not open to general nomination. Candidates are identified and selected solely by the independent adjudication panel based on documented career impact and national recognition.

Selection Process

A Rigorous, Transparent, and Independent Process

1
Nomination
Nationwide nominations open for 30 days in June. Clear eligibility criteria and structured submission templates ensure consistency and documentation integrity.
2
Screening
All submissions are reviewed for completeness, eligibility, and documentation standards. Incomplete or ineligible nominations are respectfully declined.
3
Evaluation
The independent adjudication panel assesses shortlisted nominations against published criteria — impact, ethics, service, and innovation — with complete independence from sponsors.
4
Validation
Claims and credentials are verified through official channels. Background checks are completed and documented before any nomination is finalised.
5
Final Selection
The adjudication panel convenes for final deliberation. Collective decision-making ensures no single reviewer's bias determines outcomes.
6
Celebration
Recipients are honoured at the nationally broadcast Capital Health Awards ceremony in December — a moment of lasting professional recognition.

Ethics & Fairness Statement

The Capital Health Awards operates under a strict code of ethics that protects the integrity of every selection. No sponsor, partner, media organisation, or external stakeholder may influence any nomination or adjudication outcome. All panel members are required to declare and manage conflicts of interest.

Every award recipient is selected solely on the basis of documented evidence, peer recognition, and professional merit — assessed against clearly published criteria. The Academy is committed to full post-cycle evaluation reports that document process compliance and outcomes.
